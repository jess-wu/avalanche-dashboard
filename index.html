Key Patterns & Findings
📍
BC dominates at 91%. 10 of 11 incidents occurred in British Columbia. The sole outlier was Otish Mountains, QC — an unusual location for a fatal avalanche incident.
🏔️
Every single incident was a slab avalanche. 100% slab-type. Human-triggered in most cases (skier, snowmobiler, or remote trigger). No loose snow fatals this season.
🧭
NE aspect is the danger zone. 5 of 9 incidents with aspect data were on northeast-facing slopes — typically wind-loaded and shadowed, creating persistent weak layer conditions.
📅
February–March peak. 8 of 11 incidents occurred across just 6 weeks in Feb–Mar 2026. The season opened with one incident in late December, then escalated sharply.
⚠️
Considerable danger accounts for most incidents. 4 incidents at Considerable, 2 at High. Considerably-rated days are high-traffic days — people still go out, and it's clearly still deadly.
👥
Larger groups don't guarantee survival. The Knauss Mountain incident had a group of 4 with 3 fatalities — the highest death toll of the season. Small groups (2–3) were most common, with 1 fatality each.
🏂
Snowmobiling & backcountry skiing tied at top. 4 incidents each. Mechanized skiing (heli/cat) contributed 2, including the season's worst single incident (Knauss Mountain, 3 fatalities).
📈
Season is significantly worse than 2024/25. 11 incidents vs 5 the prior year — a 120% increase. 16 fatalities this season vs approximately 7 last season.
